## üìã **REGULATORY COMPLIANCE LANDSCAPE**

### **Industry-Specific Requirements**

Different industries face varying regulatory requirements that shape security compliance obligations and implementation strategies.

#### Healthcare Compliance (HIPAA)

Healthcare organizations must implement specific safeguards for protected health information including administrative, physical, and technical safeguards with detailed documentation requirements.

#### Financial Services (PCI DSS, SOX)

Financial institutions face multiple overlapping compliance requirements including payment card data protection, financial reporting accuracy, and customer data protection standards.

#### Government and Defense (FedRAMP, FISMA)

Government contractors and agencies must meet federal security requirements including specific control implementations and extensive security documentation.

### **International Privacy Regulations**

Global privacy regulations create complex compliance requirements for organizations operating across multiple jurisdictions.

#### GDPR Implementation

European privacy regulations require specific data protection measures including privacy by design, data minimization, and individual rights protection with significant penalty structures.

#### Regional Privacy Laws

Various regional privacy laws create additional compliance requirements that must be understood and implemented appropriately for organizational operations.

#### Cross-Border Data Transfer Requirements

International data transfers require specific compliance measures and documentation to ensure appropriate protection levels across jurisdictions.

### **Control Framework Selection**

Organizations must select appropriate security control frameworks that align with regulatory requirements and business operations.

#### NIST Cybersecurity Framework

The NIST framework provides structured approach to cybersecurity risk management through identify, protect, detect, respond, and recover functions.

#### ISO 27001 Implementation

International security management standards provide comprehensive approach to information security management systems with certification requirements.

#### SOC 2 Compliance

Service organization controls provide framework for service provider security, particularly important for cloud services and outsourced operations.

### **Gap Analysis and Remediation**

Systematic assessment of current security practices against compliance requirements identifies specific areas requiring improvement or documentation.

#### Current State Assessment

Comprehensive evaluation of existing security controls, documentation, and processes provides baseline for compliance gap identification.

#### Risk-Based Prioritization

Compliance gaps should be prioritized based on regulatory risk, business impact, and implementation complexity to optimize remediation efforts.

#### Remediation Planning and Tracking

Systematic remediation planning ensures compliance gaps are addressed systematically with appropriate resource allocation and timeline management.
