# Decision Making

## Overview

This document outlines the decision-making processes and frameworks for team collaboration. It provides structured approaches to ensure decisions are made efficiently, transparently, and with appropriate involvement from stakeholders.

## Decision-Making Principles

### Core Principles

- **Transparency**: All decisions and their rationale should be documented and accessible
- **Inclusivity**: Relevant stakeholders should have opportunity to contribute
- **Timeliness**: Decisions should be made within reasonable timeframes
- **Accountability**: Clear ownership and responsibility for decisions and outcomes
- **Reversibility**: Consider if decisions can be reversed or adjusted based on new information

### Decision Quality Factors

- **Information Quality**: Decisions based on accurate, complete, and timely information
- **Stakeholder Alignment**: Consideration of all affected parties
- **Risk Assessment**: Understanding of potential consequences and mitigation strategies
- **Resource Impact**: Clear understanding of resource requirements and constraints

## Decision-Making Framework

### Decision Categories

#### Type 1: Reversible Decisions (Two-Way Doors)

- **Characteristics**: Can be easily reversed if wrong
- **Process**: Fast, individual or small team decision
- **Authority**: Delegated to closest to the work
- **Documentation**: Light documentation, communicate outcome

#### Type 2: Irreversible Decisions (One-Way Doors)

- **Characteristics**: Difficult or impossible to reverse
- **Process**: Careful analysis, broader consultation
- **Authority**: Requires senior approval
- **Documentation**: Comprehensive documentation required

## Decision-Making Processes

### Standard Decision Process

#### 1. Problem Definition

- Clearly articulate the decision that needs to be made
- Define success criteria and constraints
- Identify stakeholders and decision timeline

#### 2. Information Gathering

- Collect relevant data and expert opinions
- Research similar decisions and outcomes
- Identify assumptions and knowledge gaps

#### 3. Option Generation

- Brainstorm potential solutions
- Consider creative alternatives
- Include "do nothing" as an option when appropriate

#### 4. Evaluation

- Assess options against defined criteria
- Conduct risk analysis for each option
- Consider implementation feasibility

#### 5. Decision

- Select the best option based on evaluation
- Document decision rationale
- Plan for implementation and monitoring

#### 6. Implementation

- Execute the decision with clear ownership
- Monitor outcomes and adjust as needed
- Learn from results for future decisions

### Fast Track Process (High Urgency)

#### Abbreviated Steps

1. **Quick Assessment** (15 minutes)

   - Define the urgent decision needed
   - Identify immediately available information
   - Determine minimum viable consultation

2. **Rapid Consultation** (30 minutes)

   - Consult key stakeholders quickly
   - Gather essential perspectives
   - Identify critical risks

3. **Expedited Decision** (15 minutes)
   - Make decision with available information
   - Document key assumptions
   - Plan for review and adjustment

### Emergency Protocol (Crisis Decisions)

#### Immediate Response

- Designated decision maker has full authority
- Communicate decision to team immediately
- Focus on stabilization and damage limitation

#### Post-Crisis Review

- Conduct decision retrospective within 48 hours
- Document lessons learned
- Update emergency procedures if needed

### Decision Criteria Framework

#### Weighted Scoring Model

1. **Define Criteria** (examples):

   - Business value (weight: 30%)
   - Technical feasibility (weight: 25%)
   - Resource requirements (weight: 20%)
   - Risk level (weight: 15%)
   - Timeline impact (weight: 10%)

2. **Score Options** (1-5 scale)
3. **Calculate Weighted Scores**
4. **Select Highest Scoring Option**

## Communication Protocols

### Decision Documentation

#### Required Elements

- **Decision Statement**: Clear description of what was decided
- **Context**: Background and problem being solved
- **Options Considered**: Alternative approaches evaluated
- **Rationale**: Why this option was chosen
- **Stakeholders**: Who was involved in the decision
- **Implementation Plan**: Next steps and ownership
- **Success Metrics**: How success will be measured
- **Review Date**: When decision will be evaluated

### Stakeholder Communication

#### Announcement Template

```
DECISION: [Brief description]
CONTEXT: [Background information]
RATIONALE: [Key reasons for decision]
IMPACT: [Who/what is affected]
TIMELINE: [Implementation schedule]
NEXT STEPS: [Action items and owners]
QUESTIONS: [Contact for clarifications]
```

#### Communication Channels

- **High Impact**: All-hands meeting + written summary
- **Medium Impact**: Team meeting + email/Slack
- **Low Impact**: Email/Slack notification

## Conflict Resolution

### When Decisions Are Disputed

#### Step 1: Clarify the Disagreement

- Identify specific points of contention
- Understand underlying concerns
- Separate facts from opinions

#### Step 2: Facilitate Discussion

- Create safe space for all perspectives
- Focus on interests, not positions
- Seek common ground

#### Step 3: Escalation Path

1. **Team Discussion**: Attempt team consensus
2. **Subject Matter Expert**: Consult domain expert
3. **Management Review**: Escalate to next level
4. **Executive Decision**: Final authority when needed

### Appeal Process

#### Grounds for Appeal

- New information that significantly changes the situation
- Process was not followed correctly
- Stakeholder concerns were not adequately considered

#### Appeal Procedure

1. Submit written appeal within 5 business days
2. Include new information or process concerns
3. Review by independent party
4. Final decision communicated within 3 business days
