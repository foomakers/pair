# Content Guidelines

## Introduction

Content guidelines establish standards for creating, managing, and maintaining consistent, high-quality content across user interfaces and digital products. These guidelines ensure that all content serves user needs effectively while maintaining brand consistency, accessibility standards, and optimal user experience outcomes.

## Scope

### In Scope

- Writing style and tone guidelines
- Content structure and formatting standards
- Accessibility content requirements
- Content creation workflows and processes
- Quality assurance and review procedures
- Content governance and maintenance
- Cross-platform content consistency
- Localization and internationalization guidelines
- Content performance measurement
- Legal and compliance content considerations

### Out of Scope

- SEO strategy and optimization
- Marketing content creation
- Sales copywriting techniques
- Technical documentation authoring
- Customer service script development

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Voice and Tone Framework

**Brand Voice Characteristics**

- **Professional**: Knowledgeable and competent
- **Friendly**: Approachable and helpful
- **Clear**: Simple and understandable
- **Reliable**: Consistent and trustworthy
- **Inclusive**: Welcoming to all users

**Tone Adaptation by Context**

```
Content Context Tone Matrix:
├── Onboarding → Encouraging and supportive
├── Task Completion → Clear and efficient
├── Error States → Helpful and reassuring
├── Success States → Celebratory and positive
├── Complex Features → Patient and instructional
└── Critical Actions → Serious and clear
```

### Language Standards

**Clarity Principles**

- Use active voice whenever possible
- Choose simple words over complex ones
- Keep sentences concise and focused
- Avoid jargon and technical language
- Write for your audience's expertise level

**Consistency Requirements**

- Standardized terminology usage
- Consistent capitalization rules
- Uniform punctuation styles
- Regular formatting patterns
- Stable navigation language

## Content Structure and Organization

### Information Architecture

**Content Hierarchy**

```
Content Structure Levels:
├── H1: Page/Section Title (One per page)
├── H2: Major Section Headers
├── H3: Sub-section Headers
├── H4: Minor Sub-sections
├── Body Text: Paragraph content
├── Lists: Organized information
└── Captions: Supporting details
```

**Progressive Disclosure**

- Present essential information first
- Layer additional details appropriately
- Use expandable sections for optional content
- Provide clear navigation between levels
- Maintain logical information flow

### Content Types and Formats

**Interface Content Types**

- **Headlines**: Page and section titles
- **Body Text**: Explanatory and instructional content
- **Microcopy**: Buttons, labels, and short instructions
- **Messages**: Notifications, alerts, and feedback
- **Navigation**: Menu items and wayfinding text

**Formatting Standards**

- Consistent bullet point styles
- Standardized number formats
- Uniform date and time display
- Regular link formatting
- Systematic table structures

## Accessibility Content Requirements

### Plain Language Standards

**Readability Guidelines**

- Target 8th-grade reading level
- Use common vocabulary
- Define technical terms when necessary
- Structure content with clear headings
- Provide summaries for complex information

**Sentence and Paragraph Structure**

- Maximum 20 words per sentence
- One main idea per sentence
- Maximum 3 sentences per paragraph
- Use transitional phrases
- Maintain logical flow

### Inclusive Language Practices

**Accessibility Language**

- Person-first language usage
- Avoiding ableist terminology
- Clear and direct instructions
- Cultural sensitivity awareness
- Gender-neutral language preference

**Alternative Content Formats**

- Image alt text requirements
- Video caption standards
- Audio transcript provision
- Document summary creation
- Complex information simplification

## Content Creation Workflows

### Content Development Process

**Planning Phase**

1. **Content Audit**: Review existing content
2. **User Research**: Understand audience needs
3. **Content Strategy**: Define goals and approach
4. **Information Architecture**: Structure content organization
5. **Content Planning**: Create content calendar and assignments

**Creation Phase**

1. **Draft Creation**: Initial content development
2. **Internal Review**: Team feedback and iteration
3. **Subject Matter Expert Review**: Technical accuracy validation
4. **Legal Review**: Compliance and risk assessment
5. **Accessibility Review**: Inclusive design validation

**Publication Phase**

1. **Final Editing**: Grammar and style polishing
2. **Quality Assurance**: Final content testing
3. **Stakeholder Approval**: Final sign-off process
4. **Publication**: Content deployment
5. **Post-Publication Monitoring**: Performance tracking

### Content Collaboration

**Team Roles and Responsibilities**

- **Content Strategist**: Overall strategy and guidelines
- **UX Writer**: Interface copy and microcopy
- **Content Designer**: Content structure and presentation
- **Editor**: Grammar, style, and consistency
- **Accessibility Specialist**: Inclusive content review

**Review and Approval Workflows**

```
Content Review Process:
├── Draft Creation (UX Writer)
├── Peer Review (Content Team)
├── Design Review (Design Team)
├── Legal Review (Legal Team)
├── Accessibility Review (A11y Specialist)
├── Stakeholder Approval (Product Owner)
└── Final Publication (Content Manager)
```
